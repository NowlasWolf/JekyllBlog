Wolves are very common in gaming. Most of the time you may see them as wild enemies in RPGs, but there are some stand out others.

Lets look through some of them!

Wolf Link - The Legend of Zelda

This half counts but counts all the same. Wolf link is a form that the hero Link takes when exposed to the twilight in The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess.

This quickly becomes hindrance as much as it becomes a valuble tool for Link since he has hightened senses and a more mobile frame for the exchange of no longer being able to use any of his other items.

This feature in Twilight Princess was always controversial since segments you had to play as wolf link were often slow and tedious. Despite that, he remains a popular character in the Zelda series, getting an Amiibo figure and being added as an extra feature in The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild.

The Great Grey Wolf Sif

Sif - Dark Souls

Sif is a boss in Dark Souls, and probably the saddest one at that. Sif gaurds the grave of Artorias the Abysswalker who Sif was partner to. After he was swallowed by the abyss Sif took to gaurd his grave so that it would not happen to anyone else.

Despite technically being an enemy to Sif, you need Artorias' ring to continue and so Sif defends the grave. As Sif nears death their movements are slowed with a limp until you finally finish the fight.

A bittersweet ending to what truely is, a good boy, or girl, it is never defined.

Midday Form Lycanroc

Lycanroc - Pokemon

For as long as the Pokemon series has ran I was always suprised and disapointed a wolf pokemon was never around. There were really close ones but none based directly off of a wolf. Until Gen 7 gave us Lycanroc.

Lycanroc is semi special in that it has a few different forms depending on when and what version it evolves on. It has 3 forms: Midday, Midnight, and Dusk.

Pokemon decided to even show how sorry they were for never having any other wolf pokemon so they made the legendaries for Gen 8 two wolves!

Amaterasu - Okami

Saving the best for last, and my personal favorite, is non-other than Amaterasu from Okami. Often called "Ammy" by her companion Issun, she is the incarnation of the goddess Amaterasu in wolf form.

As a god you have powers to affect the world around you. This power manifests as a brush and allows Ammy to solve problems and fight with a versitile set of brush strokes she can use.

Okami came out at a very close time to The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess and was not only compared to it with it's wolf protaginists but also that it's gameplay is inpired a lot by the Zelda series.

One of my favorite pieces of trivia for this game comes in its title, it's a pun! "Okami" is the word for wolf in japanese, but how it is written out there reads more like "O-Kami" which better translates to "Great God" Pretty great right?
